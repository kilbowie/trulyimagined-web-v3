## Extended Value Proposition (2 minutes)

### The Problem
AI can now recreate human voices, faces, and performances with remarkable accuracy. This creates an existential challenge for actors:
- Unauthorized usage with no tracking or compensation
- No infrastructure to establish ownership or enforce consent
- Legal and ethical uncertainty around AI-generated content
- Loss of control over one's own digital identity

### Our Solution
Truly Imagined provides the foundational **rights infrastructure** for the AI era:

1. **Identity Registry** — A global, verified registry where actors establish ownership of their digital identity
2. **Consent Ledger** — An immutable audit trail of all permission decisions and usage approvals  
3. **Licensing Control** — Actor-owned rules for how, when, and where their identity can be licensed

### The Outcome
- Actors regain control over their digital identity
- Usage is tracked, transparent, and compensated
- The industry gains a compliance framework it desperately needs
- Trust is established between actors, agencies, and creators

## Common Objections & Responses

### "I already have contracts that protect me"
Contracts are important, but they need infrastructure to enforce them. Truly Imagined provides the registry and audit trail that makes your rights enforceable in AI systems.

### "What if I don't want AI to use me at all?"
That's your right. Registering establishes a record of non-consent, which is just as important as consent. It clarifies your position.

### "Will agencies lose control of their clients?"
No — agencies can manage preferences and review requests on behalf of clients. This empowers agencies to protect their clients better.

### "Isn't this too early? AI isn't widespread yet"
AI-generated content is already being used. The industry needs infrastructure **now** — before bad practices become standard.

### "How do I know you won't misuse my data?"
We never use or share your data without explicit consent. Security and privacy are foundational. We're building the trust layer — we can't compromise on trust.
